Privacy Policy for Arvo Bot

Last Updated: May 18, 2025

This Privacy Policy outlines how Arvo Bot ("Arvo", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and safeguards your information when you add Arvo to your Discord server or interact with its services, including its associated website (arvobot.xyz).

1. Information We Collect

Arvo is designed to enhance server management and staff coordination. To provide its services, Arvo may collect and store the following types of information:

  • Server Information: When Arvo is added to a Discord server, we store the server's ID (Guild ID) and name to identify it.
  • Configuration Data: Settings configured by server administrators via the /setup command, such as the designated log channel ID and command-specific role permissions, are stored on a per-server basis.
  • User Information (for commands): When you interact with Arvo's commands (e.g., submitting a form like bus assignments, or using setup commands), we collect your Discord User ID and username/tag to attribute actions, for logging purposes, and to facilitate command functionality (like DMing you).
  • Data Submitted Through Forms: Information you voluntarily provide when using Arvo's form-based commands (e.g., staff reports, bus assignments) is collected and stored to be logged in the configured channel and potentially viewed via the authenticated web interface.
  • Web View Authentication Data: If you log in to the Arvo web view (arvobot.xyz) using Discord OAuth2, we receive your Discord User ID, username, and a list of guilds you are in. This is used solely to verify your identity and check if you have the required role in the target server to view protected content. Access tokens from OAuth2 are stored in your browser's session and are not persistently stored by Arvo long-term for this purpose.
  • Bot Operational Logs: Arvo may log errors, command usage statistics (anonymized where possible), and other operational data to maintain and improve its services. These logs do not typically contain personal message content unless it's part of a form submission.

Arvo does **not** routinely log or store the content of general user messages in your server channels, outside of specific data submitted through its commands or forms.

2. How We Use Your Information

The information collected is used for the following purposes:

  • To provide, operate, and maintain Arvo's features and functionalities within your Discord server.
  • To store and apply server-specific configurations as set by administrators.
  • To log actions and form submissions to designated channels within your server.
  • To authenticate authorized personnel for accessing specific data via the Arvo web view.
  • To monitor bot performance, diagnose issues, and improve Arvo's services.
  • To respond to your support requests or inquiries.

3. Data Storage and Security

Server configurations (like log channel IDs and command permissions) and data from form submissions are currently stored in an **in-memory database** while the bot is operational. This means that if the bot restarts (e.g., due to an update or a hosting issue), this specific data may be lost unless a persistent storage solution is implemented in the future.

We take reasonable measures to protect the information we handle, but no online service can be 100% secure. The bot operates using the Discord API and is hosted on Render, which have their own security measures.

4. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal identification information or server-specific configuration data to others.

Arvo interacts with the following third-party services:

  • Discord API: Essential for the bot's operation. Arvo's use of the Discord API is subject to Discord's Developer Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.
  • Render (Hosting): Arvo is hosted on Render. Render's privacy policy applies to the data processed on their platform.

5. User Rights and Data Control

As a Discord bot, much of the data control resides within Discord itself and your server settings:

  • You can remove Arvo from your server at any time, which will stop further data collection for that server.
  • Server administrators can manage Arvo's permissions within their server.
  • Data logged by Arvo to your server channels can be managed (e.g., deleted) by users with appropriate Discord permissions in those channels.
  • For data related to your web view login session, you can log out via the website.

Due to the current in-memory storage for configurations and form submissions, specific data deletion requests for this transient data are not applicable in the same way as with persistent databases. If persistent storage is implemented, this section will be updated.

6. Children's Privacy

Arvo is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 13 (or the relevant age of digital consent in your country). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any significant changes by announcing them in our support server or through other appropriate channels. Your continued use of Arvo after such changes constitutes your acceptance of the new Privacy Policy.
